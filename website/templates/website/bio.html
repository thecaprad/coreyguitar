Bio

A fast description of my years.

Growing up

I grew up in Illinois, born in the year of the Gameboy. My favorite things to do have always been playing with computers and guitars.

I was lucky to attend a public high school that had a wonderful arts program. I did everything from writing music for school plays, playing drums in the marching band, being in the pit for plenty of musicals, and being a part of a (now viral) silly talent show performance of the Halo theme song.

Schooling

I completed a BFA in jazz guitar performance at the Univertsity of Michigan. While there, I explored my desire to play guitar in musical theatre pit orchestras. UM has a fantastic musical theatre department, and I was in every show or cabaret that would have me.

Some viral videos were made during this time:

  • “A Very Potter Sequel” — Team Starkid
  • “Sunny” — Ann Street Soul

LA

In 2012 after college graduation, I moved to Los Angeles for a job with an iPad-based sheet music startup called Chromatik. It was the early days of sheet music on tablets. I worked in QA, marketing, and ultimately ran our sheet music production team.

At the time, my college band had gone viral on Reddit for our cover of the soul classic “Sunny.” From that attention, we successfully funded and completed our self-titled EP, Ann Street Soul.

Chicago

After a year in LA, I decided to move to Chicago where there is a much larger musical theatre scene/market. (And for my wife and I to be closer to our families!) I continued working for Chromatik remotely for a few years longer.

After years of playing shows in small houses (and high schools!) I broke into the Union scene, and I’ve been lucky to be a part of some wonderful productions. A highlight to date was being a regular sub in the Chicago production of Hamilton. (For lead guitarist Felton Offard.)

Soundslice

In 2015 I left Chromatik to join Soundslice as the content director.

If you haven’t seen it, Soundslice is a web-based platform that lets you sync sheet music with real recordings. It’s an incredible tool for self study and teaching — it is used by some big organizations including TrueFire, Premiere Guitar and the Berklee School of Music.

Because I work directly with so many artists via Soundslice, I occasionally am mistakenly called the founder of the company, which is not true! Soundslice was by founded by Adrian Holovaty and PJ Macklin. You can call me employee #3, though I am a proud co-owner.

These days

I still work at Soundslice and have been growing in my web development chops. I’m quite comfortable with Python, Django, HTML, CSS, Javascript and PostgreSQL.

You can usually find me at the Irish music session on Tuesday nights at the Galway Arms in Chicago.
